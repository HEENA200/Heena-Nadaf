Mercedes-Benz Classic

Overview

Manufacturer: Mercedes-Benz

Production: Various models produced between 1901 and present

Notable Models: 300 SL Gullwing, Mercedes-Benz W196, 600 Pullman, SSK

Assembly: Stuttgart, Germany (historical models)

Body and Chassis

Class: Luxury cars, sports cars, sedans, and roadsters

Body style: Varies by model – coupe, sedan, roadster

Layout: Front-engine, rear-wheel drive (most models)

Powertrain

Engine options: Inline-6, V8, V12, and more, depending on model

Power output: Varies by model – classic models like the 300 SL produced 215 hp

Transmission: 4-speed manual, 5-speed automatic (varies by model)

Dimensions

Wheelbase, Length, Width, and Height vary significantly depending on the model.

Example (Mercedes-Benz 300 SL):

Wheelbase: 2,400 mm (94.5 in)

Length: 4,520 mm (177.9 in)

Width: 1,790 mm (70.5 in)

Height: 1,300 mm (51.2 in)

Curb weight: Approximately 1,295 kg (2,855 lb)

Performance

0–100 km/h (0–62 mph): 8.8 seconds (for 300 SL)

Top speed: 260 km/h (162 mph) (for 300 SL)

Features: Iconic gullwing doors (300 SL), timeless design, advanced engineering for its era
